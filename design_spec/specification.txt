1. Executive summary
This document defines the complete UX/UI specification for the Morph Presales CRM system. Morph operates in the interior design and fit-out space for real estate, engaging with homebuyers after they have booked a property (flat, apartment, villa, or commercial space). The presales team is the first point of contact — they reach out to buyers, understand their interior design needs, qualify them based on property details and possession timeline, and hand off qualified leads to the sales team for site visits and design consultations.
The system serves two primary user roles: the Presales Executive (who makes outbound calls, captures lead data, and qualifies homebuyers) and the Presales Manager (who reviews qualified leads, approves handoffs, and monitors team performance). Both roles report up to the CRM Head, who has oversight across all regions.
Key design principle
Every screen in this system is optimised for speed-to-contact. In interior design, the homebuyer who just booked a flat is the most receptive to an interiors conversation. A competitor calling first — even by 24 hours — can capture the deal. Every UI decision prioritises reducing time between lead arrival and first human contact.
1.1 System overview — 8 core pages
The Morph Presales CRM consists of eight distinct pages, each designed for a specific workflow:
Login and role selection  — Authentication with role-based access (Executive vs Manager)
Dashboard  — Daily snapshot with KPIs, priority calls, and possession wave forecast
Lead queue  — Filterable list of all leads with status, region, and project grouping
Lead detail and call interface  — Full lead record with property data, call form, live scoring, and disposition
Lead creation (manual)  — Form for adding walk-in, referral, or expo leads not from builder lists
Manager review queue  — Approval workflow for qualified leads awaiting handoff to sales
Pipeline and analytics  — Funnel metrics, builder-wise conversion, regional performance, exec scorecards
Settings and administration  — Region mapping, builder project master, team management, notification rules

UX recommendation: Morning briefing mode
Add a “Morning briefing” button that generates a 30-second auto-scrolling summary: total new leads since yesterday, top 3 priority calls, any leads about to breach SLA, and team performance summary. This replaces the need to manually scan the dashboard and gets the executive calling within 2 minutes of login.
4. Page 3 — Lead queue
The lead queue is the primary workspace for the presales executive. It displays all assigned leads in a filterable, sortable list view optimised for rapid scanning and call initiation.
4.1 Layout structure
The page has three zones:
Filter bar (top):  Horizontal strip with status filter pills (All, Hot, Warm, Nurture, No Answer), region dropdown, project dropdown, source filter (Builder list, Channel partner, Expo, Digital), and a search box for name/phone/project.
Lead list (centre):  Scrollable card list. Each card is a single row showing all critical data at a glance. Cards are grouped by status with section headers.
Quick stats sidebar (right):  Fixed sidebar showing today’s progress: calls made / target, leads qualified today, avg call duration, and next scheduled follow-up countdown timer.

UX critical: Auto-save during calls
The call form MUST auto-save every 10 seconds. If the browser crashes, the network drops, or the executive accidentally navigates away during a call, all captured data must be preserved. Show a subtle “Saved” indicator with timestamp. Never lose call data — this is the single biggest usability risk in a call-centre interface.
5.4 Right panel — History and context
This panel provides the executive with all historical context about the lead. It has four collapsible sections:
Call history
Chronological list of all previous calls with: date/time, duration, outcome (connected/no answer/busy), and a summary of notes taken. If call recording is enabled, a play button appears next to each entry.
Drip engagement
Shows which automated emails/WhatsApp messages the lead has received and their engagement: opened, clicked link, replied. Highlights: “Opened kitchen design email 3 days ago” — this gives the executive a natural conversation starter.
Builder project intelligence
Auto-populated contextual information about the property: builder reputation notes, known construction delays, typical layout variations for this project, nearby Morph completed projects (for reference/site visit), and any builder partnership terms.
Competitor intelligence
If the lead mentioned competitors, this section shows: competitor name, typical pricing in this area, known strengths/weaknesses, and suggested talking points for the sales team. This data is maintained centrally and auto-populated.

7.2 Manager action: Approve
When the manager taps “Approve”, the system:
Creates a handoff packet with all lead data, property details, call notes, and score breakdown.
Auto-assigns to the Sales Manager for the lead’s region based on the region mapping in settings.
Sends a push notification (and email) to the Sales Manager with the lead summary.
Updates the lead status from “Pending review” to “Handoff complete”.
Notifies the originating Presales Executive that their lead was approved.
Starts a 24-hour SLA timer for the Sales team to make first contact.
7.3 Manager action: Return
When the manager taps “Return”:
A required text field appears for the return reason (e.g., “Score seems high — did you confirm budget directly with the buyer?”, “Possession date doesn’t match builder records — please verify”).
The lead returns to the executive’s queue with a “Returned by manager” flag and the reason displayed prominently.
The executive must address the concern and re-submit, or change the disposition.
UX recommendation: Swipe to approve (mobile)
When mobile access is enabled, the review queue should support swipe gestures: swipe right to approve, swipe left to return. This allows the manager to process the queue during commutes or between meetings without needing to tap small buttons. A confirmation modal prevents accidental approvals.

11. Mobile PWA recommendations (Phase 2)
The current specification notes that presales has NO mobile access. This is the single biggest operational gap. Below is the recommended mobile strategy.
11.1 Why mobile is critical for interior design presales
Builder site visits: Presales executives attend builder events and project launches. They meet buyers face-to-face but cannot log leads until they return to their desk.
Property expos: Morph has stalls at expos across all 10 regions. Executives collect 20–30 buyer contacts per event and currently write them on paper.
Manager mobility: The presales manager visits builder offices, attends partner meetings, and travels between regions. Without mobile, the review queue stalls.
Speed advantage: A competitor with mobile lead capture can call the buyer on the same day. Morph’s executives must wait until the next business day.
11.2 Recommended mobile screens (priority order)
Quick lead capture:  A 4-field form (name, phone, project, source) that takes 20 seconds to submit. Used at expos and events.
Lead queue (simplified):  Today’s calls with swipe-to-call. No filters or sorting — just the priority list.
Manager approve/return:  Swipe right to approve, left to return. Each card shows the lead summary and score.
Dashboard KPIs:  Read-only view of the 4 metric cards and today’s priority calls.
11.3 Offline capability
For property expos and builder sites with poor connectivity, the PWA should support offline lead capture. Leads entered offline are queued locally and auto-synced when connectivity returns. A subtle indicator shows “3 leads pending sync”.
Critical: Mobile is a Phase 2 must-have
Based on the competitive landscape in Indian interior design (HomeLane, Livspace, and Design Cafe all have mobile-enabled sales teams), Morph’s lack of mobile access for presales is a measurable disadvantage. Every day of delay increases the risk of losing builder-sourced leads to faster competitors. Recommend launching the mobile PWA within 60 days of the web platform going live.

12.2 Builder list integration
For high-volume builder partnerships, explore API integration where the builder’s CRM pushes new bookings directly into Morph’s system. This eliminates the manual CSV upload step and ensures leads arrive in real-time — within minutes of the buyer signing the booking form.
12.3 WhatsApp Business API
Integrate WhatsApp Business API for: automated drip messages, appointment confirmations, design sharing, and two-way chat. WhatsApp is the dominant communication channel for Indian homebuyers and using it systematically (rather than personal WhatsApp) ensures message tracking, compliance, and scalability.
12.4 Sales feedback loop
When the sales team marks a site visit outcome (won/lost/postponed), this must feed back to the presales executive and the analytics dashboard. Specifically:
Win:  Executive gets credit. The lead’s qualification data is used to train the scoring algorithm (reinforce what “good” looks like).
Loss:  Reason is captured (price too high, competitor won, buyer postponed, bad experience). Patterns are analysed: if a specific executive’s leads lose 50% of the time due to “budget mismatch”, they need coaching on budget qualification.
Postponed:  Lead returns to the nurture pool with updated possession/timeline data. The cycle continues.
